Overview

Denali National Park, established in 1917, spans over 6 million acres of pristine wilderness in Alaska. It protects the tallest mountain in North America, Denali (20,310 feet), and vast ecosystems ranging from boreal forests to Arctic tundra.

The parkâ€™s landscape features towering peaks, glaciers, alpine meadows, and expansive taiga forests. It experiences long, harsh winters and short, cool summers.

Wildlife

Denali supports diverse wildlife including grizzly bears, wolves, caribou, moose, Dall sheep, and numerous bird species adapted to this rugged environment.

  • Winter activities such as cross-country skiing and snowshoeing.
