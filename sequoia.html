Sequoia National Park

Home to some of the largest trees on Earth and breathtaking Sierra Nevada mountain landscapes.

Overview

Sequoia National Park, established in 1890, protects vast groves of giant sequoia trees, including the famous General Sherman Tree, the largest tree by volume on the planet.

Landscape & Climate

The park features rugged mountains, deep canyons, and alpine wilderness areas. It experiences hot summers and snowy winters, supporting diverse ecosystems.

Flora & Fauna

Besides the giant sequoias, the park is home to black bears, mule deer, mountain lions, and diverse bird species in a rich forested environment.

Activities & Experiences

  • Hiking to giant sequoia groves and scenic mountain trails.
  • Exploring caves like Crystal Cave by guided tour.
  • Camping, wildlife viewing, and photography.
  • Winter sports such as snowshoeing and cross-country skiing.

Conservation Efforts

Efforts focus on protecting ancient trees, managing fire regimes, and preserving wildlife habitats amidst climate challenges.
