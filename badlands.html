Badlands National Park

A striking landscape of sharply eroded buttes, pinnacles, and spires with rich fossil beds in South Dakota.

Overview

Badlands National Park, established in 1978, protects over 244,000 acres of rugged terrain shaped by millions of years of erosion. The park is known for its dramatic geological formations and rich fossil history.

Geology & Landscape

The park features layered sedimentary rock formations, colorful deposits of clay and sandstone, deep canyons, and striking pinnacles that create a surreal and otherworldly scenery.

Wildlife & Vegetation

Despite the harsh landscape, Badlands is home to bison, bighorn sheep, prairie dogs, coyotes, and numerous bird species. The mixed-grass prairie supports native grasses and wildflowers.

Activities & Exploration

  • Hiking trails through canyons and overlooks like the Notch and Castle Trail.
  • Wildlife viewing especially around prairie dog towns and bison herds.
  • Fossil exhibits and ranger programs highlighting the park's paleontology.
  • Scenic drives on the Badlands Loop Road with multiple viewpoints.
