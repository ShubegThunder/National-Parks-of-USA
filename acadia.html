Acadia National Park

A stunning coastal refuge where rocky shores meet lush forests, offering dramatic landscapes, rich wildlife, and countless outdoor adventures.

Overview

Established in 1916, Acadia National Park protects over 49,000 acres of Maineâ€™s rugged coastline. Known for its granite peaks, dense pine forests, and scenic shorelines, the park is a beloved destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Ecology & Habitat Diversity

Acadia is home to diverse ecosystems ranging from coastal cliffs and salt marshes to spruce-fir forests. Wildlife includes black bears, moose, peregrine falcons, and a rich variety of marine life along its shores.

Wildlife Highlights

Visitors often spot white-tailed deer, bald eagles, otters, and seals. The parkâ€™s rich habitats support migratory birds and rare plant species, making it a vital ecological treasure.

Activities & Experiences

  • Explore by kayak or boat along the rugged coastline to discover hidden coves.
  • Enjoy ranger-led programs, wildlife watching, and seasonal events throughout the year.
  • Bike the historic carriage roads with picturesque stone bridges and tranquil settings.
