Canyonlands National Park

A vast, rugged wilderness of deep canyons, towering mesas, and winding rivers carved by natureâ€™s force over millions of years.

Overview

Canyonlands National Park, located in southeastern Utah, preserves a dramatic landscape sculpted by the Colorado River and its tributaries. The park covers over 337,000 acres of sandstone buttes, arches, and breathtaking vistas.

Geology & Landscape

The parkâ€™s unique geology features layered rock formations, deep canyons, natural arches, and expansive mesas. It offers endless opportunities for exploration and photography against a backdrop of rugged beauty.

Wildlife & Flora

Canyonlands supports desert-adapted wildlife including bighorn sheep, coyotes, mule deer, and a variety of birds of prey. The vegetation ranges from hardy desert shrubs to cottonwoods along riverbanks.

Activities & Experiences

  • Hiking and backpacking through diverse terrains including the famous Island in the Sky and Needles districts.
  • River rafting on the Colorado and Green Rivers for a unique perspective on the park.
  • Stargazing in one of the darkest night skies in the U.S.
  • Photography of iconic landmarks like Mesa Arch and Upheaval Dome.

Conservation Efforts

Park management focuses on preserving fragile desert ecosystems, managing visitor impact, and protecting archaeological sites dating back thousands of years.
